Voyager 2 Heliosphere Plasma Science (PLS) Solar Wind Plasma, Hourly Data

ResourceID
spase://VSPO/NumericalData/Voyager2/PLS/Heliosphere/PT1H

Description
  • Data Set Overview
  • =================

Voyager 2 Plasma Data of the Solar Wind, 1 h Averages. The Files in this Directory contain the Voyager hourly average Plasma Data. The Plasma Parameters are obtained by finding the best Fit of a Convected Isotropic Maxwellian Distribution to the Data. One Sigma Errors are typically less than 0.5% in the Speed and the Radial Velocity, VR, less than 5% for the Density and Thermal Speed, and vary greatly for the other two Velocity Components, VT and VN. Sampling Times range from 12 s to 192 s, with sampling generally more frequent early in the Mission. The Columns are:

    1. Year
    1. Day of Year, Day 1 is January 1
    1. Hour
    1. Proton Speed in km/s, the Magnitude of Velocity Vector
    1. Proton Number Density in cm^-3
    1. Proton Thermal Speed in km/s, the Proton Temperature in eV is equal to 0.0052 times the Square of the Thermal Speed
    1. Velocity Vector R Component, VR, in km/s
    1. Velocity Vector T Component, VT, in km/s, Warning: This Parameter is often not reliable after 1989
    1. Velocity Vector N Component, VN, in km/s, Warning: This Parameter is often not reliable after 1989

The Velocity Components are given in the RTN Coordinate System, where R is radially outward, T is in a Plane parallel to the Solar Equatorial Plane and positive in the Direction of Solar Rotation, and N completes the Right-handed System.

Please consult with us, or at least send Preprints, when you use these Data to prevent grievous Errors or Misconceptions, John Richardson, jdr@space.mit.edu.
